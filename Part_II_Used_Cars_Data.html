Investigation Overview¶

I will just focus on the effect of max_power and km_driven on the selling price of used cars in the dataset. Also, I will be looking into time trends of sale across regions and features of sold cars.

Dataset Overview¶

Based on various market surveys, the consulting firm has gathered a large dataset of different types of used cars across the market. The dataset is madeup of different information about used cars gotten from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shubham1kumar/usedcar-data?resource=download.

The dataset is madeup of 7906 rows and 18 columns in general.

Data Dictionary:

  • Sales_ID (Sales ID)
  • name (Name of the used car)
  • year (Year of the car purchase)
  • selling_price (Current selling price for used car)
  • km_driven (Total km driven)
  • Region (Region where it is used)
  • State or Province (State or Province where it is used)
  • City (City where it is used)
  • fuel (Fuel type)
  • seller_type (Who is selling the car)
  • transmission (Transmission type of the car)
  • transmission (Transmission type of the car)
  • owner (Owner type)
  • mileage (Mileage of the car)
  • engine (engine power)
  • max_power (max power)
  • seats (Number of seats)
  • sold (used car sold or not)

# Functions for various plots
def Hist_bin(data, x, xlabel, ylabel, title, bins):
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    plt.hist(data=data, x=x, bins=bins)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel)
    plt.ylabel(ylabel)
    plt.title(title, loc='center');
    
def Hist(data, x, xlabel, ylabel, title):
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    plt.hist(data=data, x=x)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel)
    plt.ylabel(ylabel)
    plt.title(title, loc='center');
    
def Scatter(data, x, y, xlabel, ylabel, title):
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    plt.scatter(data=data, x=x, y=y, alpha=0.2)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel)
    plt.ylabel(ylabel)
    plt.title(title, loc='center');

Distribution of Selling Price of Cars in the Dataset¶

In a bit to look at the distribution of selling price across the data set, the histogram shows that cars whose selling price is between \$30,000 and \\$1,000,000 are much in the dataset. But in order to dive deeper into the dataset, log transformation was carried out on the price axis, this later shows that the price distribution is a unimodal distribution in which the modal price is in between \$300,000 and \\$400,000. After \$1,000,000, it was oberved that the price is rising and falling in the counts.

Trends in Price of Sold Cars Over the Years¶

The trends of price across the year shows that the price of cars is increasing over the years, except in 2001, 2006, 2012, 2014 and 2018 where there is a slight fall in the price, then a great hike in price from 2018 to 2019 wih great fall in the following year 2020. The disjoint in 1995 to 1999 simply shows that there is no sales in those years.

It also shows that the South has the highest sale in 2019 which happens to be outrageous compared to that of other regions.
